# Health Copy Rules

## Core Rules
- Do not diagnose users.
- Do not create fear.
- Use gentle language.
- Encourage professional care for serious symptoms.
- Avoid medical jargon.
- Explain health terms simply.
- Use supportive, non-judgmental language.
- Avoid labeling a body, cycle, symptom, or habit as bad.
- Do not imply certainty from limited logs.

## Safe Language Patterns
- Use "may", "might", "could", and "can be worth noting".
- Say "pattern" instead of "problem" when possible.
- Say "worth discussing with a doctor" instead of "dangerous" unless emergency guidance is required.
- Say "your logged symptoms" instead of "your condition".
- Say "prediction" or "estimate" for cycle dates.

## Allowed Examples
```txt
"This pattern may be worth discussing with a doctor."
"Your symptoms were logged. Bloop will remember this context."
"Your next period may start in 5 days."
```

## Avoid
```txt
"You have PCOS."
"Your cycle is abnormal."
"You are at high risk."
```

## Serious Symptom Guidance
When symptoms are severe, sudden, persistent, or unusual for the user, encourage professional care calmly.

Example:
```txt
"If this pain is severe, sudden, or unusual for you, consider contacting a healthcare professional."
```

Do not create panic. Do not minimize symptoms. Keep the user informed and supported.

## Medical Terms
If a medical term is needed, explain it simply.

Example:
```txt
"Ovulation is when an ovary releases an egg. Some people notice discharge, mild cramps, or mood changes around this time."
```
