# Project Overview

## App Name
MyStree Soul

## Product Vision
MyStree Soul is a calm, trustworthy women health companion that helps users understand their cycle, daily symptoms, mood, sleep, and personal health records without feeling judged, rushed, or overwhelmed.

The app should make health tracking feel like a supportive ritual: simple enough for everyday use, organized enough for doctor visits, and gentle enough for moments when users may already feel worried.

## Target Users
- Women and menstruating people who want clearer cycle and symptom tracking.
- Users who forget symptom history before doctor appointments.
- Users who want a softer alternative to cold, clinical health apps.
- Users managing recurring concerns such as cramps, mood shifts, irregular timing, sleep changes, acne, fatigue, or discharge changes.
- Users who need one place for records, observations, and personal context.

## Core Promise
MyStree Soul helps users notice patterns, remember context, and feel prepared to seek care when needed. It does not diagnose. It supports reflection, logging, organization, and gentle next steps.

## Main App Sections
- Onboarding: A warm setup flow that learns goals and basic cycle context.
- Home: A daily overview with cycle timing, quick logs, and gentle insights.
- Cycle: A readable cycle view with phases, predictions, and symptom patterns.
- Vault: A private place for health records and visit notes.
- Profile: Preferences, privacy controls, cycle settings, and app personalization.
- Bloop: A floating companion for quick logging, reminders, and supportive context.

## Why The App Exists
Many users have scattered information across memory, notes, lab reports, calendars, and separate apps. MyStree Soul exists to bring that context together in a way that is organized, emotionally safe, and easy to use before, during, and after healthcare conversations.

## What The App Must Feel Like
- Calm and supportive.
- Trustworthy and privacy-aware.
- Warm, but not childish.
- Healthcare focused, but not clinical.
- Clear, readable, and light.
- Helpful without being alarmist.
- Designed for real daily use, not just first impressions.

## What The App Must Not Feel Like
- Fear-based or diagnostic.
- Overloaded with charts, jargon, or dense forms.
- Cold, sterile, or hospital-like.
- Cute in a way that reduces trust.
- Judgmental about bodies, cycles, symptoms, fertility, weight, or habits.
- Like it is replacing a doctor.
- Like a generic wellness dashboard with no women health focus.
